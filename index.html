Introduction:

The Au Pair Project, often simply referred to as the Au Pair Program, is a cultural exchange initiative that allows young people (usually aged 18-26) to live with a host family in another country, provide childcare, and immerse themselves in the host countryâ€™s culture. In the U.S., the Au Pair Program operates under the J-1 Exchange Visitor Visa Program and is administered by the U.S. Department of State. Its primary goals are to promote cultural exchange and mutual understanding between the U.S. and other countries.

Key Features of the Au Pair Program:

1. Cultural Exchange Focus: The au pair lives with a host family as a member of the household and participates in cultural and language exchange.This program provides an opportunity for au pairs to experience life in the U.S. while sharing their own culture with the host family. 2. Childcare Responsibilities: Au pairs provide up to 45 hours of childcare per week (up to 10 hours per day). This includes tasks such as preparing meals for children, helping with homework, and supervising playtime.They cannot perform non-childcare-related domestic work. 3. Educational Requirement: Au pairs are required to enroll in an accredited U.S. post-secondary educational institution and complete at least 6 credit hours (or equivalent), funded in part by the host family. 4. Program Duration: The standard program duration is 12 months, with the possibility of extending for an additional 6, 9, or 12 months. 5. Stipend and Benefits:Au pairs receive a minimum weekly stipend (set by the Department of State), free room and board, and contributions toward their educational expenses. Host families cover travel expenses for the au pair's arrival and return.

Eligibility:

- Be between the ages of 18 and 26. - Have prior childcare experience. - Be proficient in English. - Pass health and background checks.
